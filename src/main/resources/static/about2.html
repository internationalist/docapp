Dr. Swati Palit Deb
Associate Professor
Biochemistry & Molecular Biology
Microbiology & Immunology

Primary research interest of my laboratory is to reveal the fragile steps of oncogene induced cell proliferation during lung cancer and repair of lung injury that can be utilized therapeutically. At present, my laboratory has two major areas of investigation. One of our areas of interest is to investigate the dependency of lung progenitor cell replication during repair of lung injury, and to determine whether the process cooperates with oncogenic transformation of lung cells. Our other area of interest is to reveal the “Achilles heel” of DNA replication in lung cancer cells, targeting at which would destroy cancer cells overcoming their apparently robust ability to grow.

Dr. Shilpa Singh
Postdoctoral Fellow
AACR Lung Cancer Postdoctoral Research Fellow

My major research interest is to determine how and why normal cells turn to cancer cells and how to prevent this process. As a graduate student, I have investigated contributions of oncogenes (MDM2 and gain of function p53 mutation) in deregulation of normal or cancer cells, and have identified mechanisms by which oncogenic p53 mutants confer targetable dependency on DNA replication in lung cancer cells for the first time. Currently, I am investigating how oncogenic p53 mutants confer dependency on checkpoint signaling during DNA replication and mitosis of lung cancer cells and tumors. I hope to uncover novel un-explored small molecule inhibitors to therapeutically target proliferation of lung cancers with oncogenic p53 mutation without affecting the normal WT p53 containing cells..
